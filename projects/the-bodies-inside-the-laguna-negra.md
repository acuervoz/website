LA BONITA, CARACAS, VENEZUELA - Pedro Silva, a 25 year old man shares the story of how two of his friends lost their lives in the so-called "Laguna Negra" (Black Lagoon) by the residents of La Bonita.

The Black Lagoon carries a history of countless victims.

Some people say that the lagoon is between 30 and 40 meters deep. Others say it is shallow and poses no danger. What we know for sure is that the black waters of this lagoon have been the cause of warnings and legends in the society of La Bonita and La Tahona.

Here is the testimony of Pedro Silva, sole survivor of 3 people who camped together 2 months ago by the shore of the lagoon.

> "I lost my best friends there. My relatives and my family called the PNB (National Police force of Venezuela), but nothing. They hardly go near that area and even less after hearing my story. Not out of fear. They made fun in my face of my testimonial and said they didn't believe me, but still they don't explain the disappearance of the bodies." — Pedro Silva

We received testimonials from the victims' relatives confirming the PNB's attitude towards the request to search for the bodies. The PNB kept Pedro in custody for 37 days on suspicion of murdering his friends. He was released due lack of evidence in the face of the accusation.

> "People don't even come close anymore and if they do well... just know that whatever goes in those water, doesn't come out." — Pedro Silva

## The legend of the "Laguna Negra"

We spoke with a local security guard, whose identity will be kept private. He told us the legend that has been passed down through generations of his family members who have lived in the area:

> People believe that by avoiding the waters they will be safe, but the lagoon is not alone. One day it spat shadows, witches, cursed birds, or whatever you want to call them since they have many names. They know how to imitate voices very well and get your attention in the forest near the lagoon. The curious ones sentence themselves to death at the sight of them. They feel your gaze with their 6 blood-colored eyes. They are patient, they will wait as long as it takes to bring you to the cursed lagoon. The only peaceful moment they give you is the time it takes you to return home. But when you get home, be prepared to hear footsteps on the roof. It doesn't matter if it's an apartment or a house, you will hear claws scratching the ceiling night after night without stopping. Others say they will knock on your window six times every night. It doesn't matter if you don't see them, they see you all the time. If you want to live my friend, say goodbye to sleep. Those damn witches will visit you until you open your window. Just ignore them, and don't even think about telling other people what's going on with you because they'll call you crazy.

## The last night Pedro saw his friends

Pedro recalls the moment they decided to go to the lagoon. They had heard about the legends from a Venezuelan youtuber's video. Someone had recently drowned and they said his ghost was still by the lagoon, screaming for help during morning hours.

> "We listened to the legend and quickly decided to spend a night there. We prepared during the week and looked for the best place to camp for the day by the lagoon's shore." — Pedro Silva

They set up camp, played, ate and drank until late. It was early morning when the silence imposed itself. Neither the wind, nor the cars or motorcycles passing by could be heard. Not even the characteristic sound of the Antillean Coqui (rain frogs) that fills the streets of Caracas every night.

Pedro tells that the dense fog filled the space, blinding them. Only a scream broke the silence, followed by another, both from his friends.

> "I tried to follow them until I stumbled over their empty chairs thrown to the floor which suddenly filled with water." — Pedro Silva

The screaming stopped and he accepted the worst. He closed his eyes feeling the water cover his ankles. He felt a huge, heavy animal-like creature trying to pull him until 2 voices appeared in front of him. The voices of his friends, their bodies soaked, pale, muddy. Thick black water coming out from all their orifices.

> "Alive... Loneliness... Hunger..." — his friends, in unison

> "It took me a huge while to understand who was talking to me. It was the lagoon. It was alive, but I didn't understand anything. I just cried, covering my eyes. The moment I lifted my face I saw my friends fall under the water in one fell swoop. In desperation I tried to pull them out, but I couldn't get them from the shore." — Pedro Silva

Pedro confesses that having survived has been one of his greatest regrets. Try as he might, he can't stop seeing the dead stares of his friends.

> "To think that something used them as puppets makes me nauseous." — Pedro Silva

## Pedro cannot forget

Today Pedro has the habit to visit the lagoon at least once a month. He says the lagoon leaves him at peace. Every time he goes, he sees the reflection of his friends' bodies in the water next to his own. Pedro believes that the lagoon is waiting for him, leaving him to make the decision to join his friends.

> "One day I saw how the lagoon spit out some giant birds, like crows. I don't know why I keep going. I think I hope I can rescue my friend's bodies, but I still don't know what to do. How do you beat something like that?" — Pedro Silva
