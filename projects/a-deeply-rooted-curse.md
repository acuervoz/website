CANAIMA, VENEZUELA - A group of five tourists from the United States and their tour guide found themselves lost for over 2 days. A search and rescue had been issued by the local authorities who were only able to find four of the tourists. The operation has been terminated due to the lack of progress in finding the rest.

Their friend Sarah, 26 year old student, and tour guide Salvador (49) are still missing to this day. The group claims Sarah had been brutally murdered by the hands of the townsfolk. Local authorities have not been able to confirm the existence of the town the group of tourists refers to.

The four survivors spent three months detained in trials inside Venezuela before being released to their country due to the lack of evidence. As soon as the tourists arrived they gave us a call and we interviewed each one of the members hoping to bring to light their version of the story.

## Story told by the survivors

The group was formed by Miguel (27), Jean (26), Tom (27), Sarah (26) and Jayce (25) and tour guide Salvador (47). They had booked a month-long holiday to visit different parts of Venezuela: Los Roques, Margarita, Caracas, Mérida and ending in Canaima.

During their trip in Canaima, the tour guide Salvador had arranged a five hours walking tour towards the waterfall called "The Bride's Veil". They found no issues during the trip to it, however, upon the return, the group noticed Salvador uneasy. According to him "Things where not as they are supposed to be." It is worth noting GPS trackers are not used in Canaima. Tourist guides know the routes from their parents and grandparents who taught them, teaching the routes across generations.

The group recalls spending at least one hour and thirty minutes going in circles. The five hours trip turned into eight. After sundown they found a dirt road leading to a small town called "Mukoton Caylon" (Translated to: Sons of the deformed tree). Salvador was fluent in the Pemón language and suggested to take refuge in the town for the night, since it would be safer for them than sleeping in the wilderness.

> "We would have rather slept naked in the wilderness than in that cursed place." — Tom

To this day, the town has not been found on any maps of Venezuela, if you have any information please send us a note.

According to the group, the town was formed of at least 10 different "churuatas" (Circular palm huts with straw ceilings and no walls). The townsfolk were welcoming of them who, in retrospect, believe their welcome was "too nice" with a "strange intensity" in their smiles. Salvador explained the situation to one of the locals who guided the group inside the town. According to Salvador, they were welcome to spend the night.

The group settled inside a Churuata. The place had no rooms, just the empty circular shape except for a few handmade beds and bedding. Salvador outside speaking with some of the locals.

They recall feeling strange during night time, describing it as heavy, more silent than what they had gotten used to after their previous nights in Canaima. The town felt isolated from all the animals, bugs and other sounds typical of the region.

In the middle of the night, locals dressed in colorful handmade clothes woke them up with singing, drums and dances. The group, confused, had not much choice in the matter, the locals took them outside where they found a feast. The group tried asking for Salvador with their limited knowledge of Spanish but the locals only spoke Pemon. They looked around hoping to find any trace of him but he was nowhere to be seen.

They tried their best to contact someone from outside the town, but with the lack of phone signal, they gave up. Their "coping mechanism" as they called it, was thinking "this is part of the adventure" so they agreed to perform all the activities with the locals for the lack of a better choice.

At some point during the night arrived and the townsfolk guided the group outside of the small town area, entering an area they described as "dense with trees, heavy feeling and darker than the town." After walking for several minutes they reach a more open area where people danced and played music around a fire and an "oddly shaped tree." All of them described the shape the same way: "It looked too much like a person." They point out that most trees had similar shapes, and with the light from the fire, some even had the shapes of people.

Next to the fire they saw a woman around their age, covered in mud or dirt with large branches embracing her and leaves protruding from her skin.

> "I felt like I was on a trip. The situation was surreal." — Miguel

Locals offered them a thick brown soup to eat. All four of them recall the taste like dirt mixed with meat soup flavoring powder. Disgusted, they barely pushed through dinner when the locals turned their attention to Sarah, surrounding her, gifting her necklaces made from branches while using mud to paint symbols on her arms, legs and face. The group was confused but felt too scared to do something about the situation. By the time everyone settled down and started to return to the churuatas, the girl embraced by the tree was nowhere to be seen.

## She is sick

On the second day, the group woke up feeling sluggish and tired with dry skin, especially Sarah, whose skin was as rough as cardboard with parts of her bleeding a yellowish thick substance. Jayce and Miguel took her to the townsfolk, a man and an older lady smiled, taking Sarah to the house without saying a word while two men prevented Jayce and Miguel from entering.

> "We let them take her against our better judgment. Honestly, we didn't know what else to do. She was in pain and we were in panic mode." — Jayce

> "We all discussed our few options, which were all awful. We felt cornered and hopeless until Salvador miraculously returned." — Miguel

Salvador returned early in the morning with good news. He found the way back to camp. Immediately after, they told him about Sarah. He went to ask how she was doing and what they were doing to her.

> "After seeing her, he told us the change in her skin was called a 'blessing' that happens because of the exotic foods, like a detox he told us, and there was nothing to worry about. They were just treating her with ointments and special oils. But we didn't believe much of it. We were extremely worried and we all wished to have done something about it then." — Tom

During the night, Miguel and Tom snuck out of the churuata to see Sarah for themselves. They looked from afar, avoiding the men guarding the Churuata. They saw Sarah naked, covered in dirt only illuminated by the light of a few candles. Her arms were protruding small branches and leaves out of them with lumps covering most of her face.

> "She looked like a monster. She had lumps deforming her face, we couldn't believe it was her." — Miguel

The two returned to the cabin to warn the others. Salvador however, was dismissive, telling them to not worry and to be thankful for the hospitality since they might have to spend another night due to the weather. The group, despite Salvador's request, planned to leave the day after, even if they didn't know their way around.

## ~~No~~-one left behind

Next morning they woke up to the locals celebrating like the day before and with Salvador gone again. They wore the same outfits and were preparing the place for another celebration.

The tourist then divided into two groups, one to gather their essentials and the others to get Sarah. Unfortunately by the time they go to the Churuata where the locals kept Sarah, it was empty. Then they noticed the rest were empty too, with the townsfolk nowhere to be seen, except for noises coming from the location where the singing and dancing had taken place.

All of the townsfolk celebrated like the night before. They danced around someone in the center. When the tourists got closer, they noticed it was Sarah. Her body covered in mud with branches embracing her body attached or coming out from her limbs.

> "I wish to forget, we don't tell her family what we saw because they probably wouldn't believe us and besides, how would that help them?" — Miguel

The tourists were frozen in place, Jayce even puking to the sight of Sarah. It was only when everyone around the fire turned stopped dancing that they reacted. Locals started running and yelling at them. They screamed Sarah's name but unfortunately she was unresponsive. In the rush of the moment Jayce was only able to take a picture from Sarah, the last known picture of her.

They ran to meet their friends by the entrance, leaving Sarah behind.

> "'She's dead! She's dead!' I heard Miguel scream in a way I've never heard him before." — Jean

They ran and ran until exhaustion. Fortunately for them, the locals where nowhere to be seen and they managed to slow down their paced and spent the night hiding under trees. The next day they walked around, trying to make sense of the trees and compasses they had. Even though they had no sure way to go, they knew very well which direction not to go to.

> "We went as far from the south west as possible." — Tom

Fortunately, right before dawn, they were found by local authorities.

The local authorities launched an investigation to look for the town, Salvador and Sarah's remains. The authorities even suspected the group of survivors to be responsible for their disappearance. However due to lack of evidence, they were let go and each returned to their lives in the United States.

> "Nothing has been the same, I can still see those trees and Sarah becoming one, her legs seemed broken in the weirdest and most disgusting ways possible." — Miguel

The group now blames Salvador for their fate and are running a donation campaign to search and find Sarah's remains and give their family the peace they deserve.
